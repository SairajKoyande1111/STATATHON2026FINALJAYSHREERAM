## 0. What is the Comparison Module?

The **Comparison Module** is the **aggregated, cross-attack synthesis layer** of the Risk Assessment module. After the user runs any combination of the 10 individual attacks, this tab:

1. **Aggregates** the risk scores from all enabled attacks into a single composite score (the NIST Composite Risk Score shown in the header).
2. **Normalises** each attack's output to a common 0–100 scale.
3. **Cross-compares** attacks along key dimensions — threat model, protection mechanism, severity — to give NSO decision-makers a single-screen answer: *"How bad is this dataset overall, and which attack is the primary threat?"*
4. **Generates** a priority-ordered action list that de-duplicates and ranks recommendations across all attacks.
5. **Explains** what the composite score means in plain English, with contextual thresholds from NIST, GDPR EDPB, and DPDP Act 2023 standards.

> The Comparison score visible as "86.8" in the top navigation bar (from the screenshot) is this module's primary output.

---

## 1. Input: Scores from All 10 Attacks

Before computing the composite, the module **collects one normalised score per enabled attack**. The table below shows the canonical formula for each attack's score on the 0–100 scale:

| # | Attack | Score Variable | Formula | Score Range | Threshold (SAFE) |
|---|--------|---------------|---------|-------------|------------------|
| 1 | Prosecutor | `prosecutor_score` | `(num_singletons / N) × 100` | 0–100 | < 5 |
| 2 | Journalist | `journalist_score` | `re_id_risk_journalist × 100` | 0–100 | < 5 |
| 3 | Marketer | `marketer_score` | `marketer_reid_rate × 100` | 0–100 | < 5 |
| 4 | Singling Out | `singling_out_score` | `predicate_so_rate × 100` | 0–100 | < 5 |
| 5 | Inference | `inference_score` | `overall_form_a_risk × 100` | 0–100 | < 40 |
| 6 | Membership | `membership_score` | `pct_high_risk_records` | 0–100 | < 5 |
| 7 | Rec. Linkage | `rec_linkage_score` | `ECLR × 100` | 0–100 | < 5 |
| 8 | Attr. Disclose | `attr_disclose_score` | `Overall_ADR × 100` | 0–100 | < 20 |
| 9 | Differencing | `differencing_score` | `DDR × 100` | 0–100 | < 20 |
| 10 | Model Inversion | `model_inversion_score` | `MIRisk_dataset × 100` | 0–100 | < 30 |

> **Critical note on Inference score:** Inference has a different "safe" threshold (< 40) because the Form A confidence score has a non-zero floor of `1/num_SA_categories`. A "safe" dataset can legitimately score 20–35 on Inference. This must be reflected in the normalisation (Section 2.3).

## 2. Mathematical Model — Composite Score

### 2.1 Notation

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `n` | Number of **enabled** attacks (user can disable individual attacks in the left panel) |
| `Sᵢ` | Raw score for attack `i` (0–100 scale, as per Section 1) |
| `Wᵢ` | Weight for attack `i` (see Section 2.2) |
| `Nᵢ` | Normalised score for attack `i` after threshold adjustment (see Section 2.3) |
| `NIST_CRS` | NIST Composite Risk Score (the headline number, 0–100) |
| `PASS_i` | Boolean: whether attack `i` is in PASS state (score below safe threshold) |

---

### 2.2 Weighting Model

Not all attacks are equal in severity. The Comparison module uses a **severity-weighted average** rather than a simple mean. Default weights:

| Attack | Weight `Wᵢ` | Justification |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Prosecutor | 1.5 | Worst-case adversary; known-target threat; most legally significant |
| Marketer | 1.5 | Bulk re-identification; highest commercial harm at scale |
| Model Inversion | 1.5 | Directly reconstructs sensitive values; hardest to defend without privacy engineering |
| Singling Out | 1.2 | GDPR-defined primary harm; no external data needed |
| Inference | 1.2 | Bypasses k-anonymity; detects structural leakage |
| Attr. Disclose | 1.2 | Directly linked to l-diversity violation; sensitive value exposure |
| Differencing | 1.0 | Aggregate-level risk; depends on release scenario |
| Rec. Linkage | 1.0 | Bulk join threat; same formula as Prosecutor but different framing |
| Journalist | 0.8 | Journalist ≤ Prosecutor always; provides population-adjusted lower bound |
| Membership | 0.8 | Presence-only disclosure; narrower harm than re-identification |

> **Configuration note for Replit Agent:** The weights `Wᵢ` must be **configurable constants** defined at the top of the Comparison module file — not hardcoded inline. NSO staff may wish to adjust weights based on their data release policy context.

---

### 2.3 Score Normalisation (Threshold-Relative Scaling)

Because different attacks have different "safe" thresholds (some are SAFE at < 5, Inference at < 40, Model Inversion at < 30), raw scores are NOT directly comparable. A raw score of 40 on Inference is LOW risk, but a raw score of 40 on Prosecutor is CRITICAL.

**Normalise each raw score to a 0–100 "danger percentage" relative to its safe threshold:**

```
safe_threshold_i = [values from Section 1 rightmost column]
critical_threshold_i = 80  (uniform — any attack scoring above 80 raw is Critical)

Nᵢ = clip( (Sᵢ - safe_threshold_i) / (critical_threshold_i - safe_threshold_i) × 100, 0, 100 )
```

**Worked example:**
```
Inference raw score = 45
safe_threshold = 40
critical_threshold = 80

Nᵢ = (45 - 40) / (80 - 40) × 100 = 5/40 × 100 = 12.5   → LOW normalised risk

Prosecutor raw score = 45
safe_threshold = 5
critical_threshold = 80

Nᵢ = (45 - 5) / (80 - 5) × 100 = 40/75 × 100 = 53.3   → MEDIUM normalised risk
```

This makes the composite score meaningful: a score of 53 always means "roughly halfway between safe and critical" regardless of which attack generated it.

---

### 2.4 NIST Composite Risk Score (NIST_CRS)

```
NIST_CRS = (Σᵢ  Wᵢ × Nᵢ)  /  (Σᵢ Wᵢ)

Where the sum is over all ENABLED attacks i that have successfully run.
```

**Boundary conditions:**
- If NO attacks are enabled: `NIST_CRS = 0`, display "No attacks selected."
- If only 1 attack is enabled: `NIST_CRS = N₁` (normalised score of that single attack).
- If an attack is enabled but returned an error or "insufficient data": exclude it from both numerator and denominator.

**Risk level classification:**

| NIST_CRS | Risk Level | Badge Colour | Meaning |
|----------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| 0 – 24 | LOW | 🟢 Green | Dataset is reasonably well-protected across all attack types |
| 25 – 49 | MEDIUM | 🟡 Yellow | Significant vulnerabilities in multiple attack dimensions |
| 50 – 69 | HIGH | 🟠 Orange | Serious re-identification or disclosure risk; remediation required |
| 70 – 100 | CRITICAL | 🔴 Red | Dataset must NOT be released without major privacy engineering |

---

### 2.5 Pass/Fail Tally

In addition to the weighted composite, report a **simple pass/fail count** using raw scores and safe thresholds:

```
for each enabled attack i:
    PASS_i = (Sᵢ < safe_threshold_i)

Pass_count  = count(PASS_i == True)
Fail_count  = count(PASS_i == False)
Pass_rate   = Pass_count / n × 100
```

The Pass/Fail tally appears as: **"10/10 attacks run · 2/10 PASS · 8/10 FAIL"** directly below the NIST_CRS gauge.

---

### 2.6 Worst-Case Single Attack Score

```
Worst_Score   = max(Nᵢ for all enabled i)
Worst_Attack  = argmax(Nᵢ)
```

This is reported as: **"Highest risk: [Worst_Attack] ([Worst_Score])"** in the summary panel.

## 3. Full Comparison Algorithm (Step by Step)

```python
def run_comparison(attack_results, enabled_attacks, user_k, user_l, user_t):
    """
    attack_results: dict mapping attack_name -> result_dict from each attack module
    enabled_attacks: list of attack names the user has checked in the left panel
    """

    WEIGHTS = {
        'Prosecutor':     1.5,
        'Journalist':     0.8,
        'Marketer':       1.5,
        'Singling Out':   1.2,
        'Inference':      1.2,
        'Membership':     0.8,
        'Rec. Linkage':   1.0,
        'Attr. Disclose': 1.2,
        'Differencing':   1.0,
        'Model Inversion':1.5,
    }

    SAFE_THRESHOLDS = {
        'Prosecutor':     5,
        'Journalist':     5,
        'Marketer':       5,
        'Singling Out':   5,
        'Inference':      40,
        'Membership':     5,
        'Rec. Linkage':   5,
        'Attr. Disclose': 20,
        'Differencing':   20,
        'Model Inversion':30,
    }

    CRITICAL_THRESHOLD = 80  # uniform upper anchor for normalisation

    # Step 1: Extract raw scores from each enabled attack
    raw_scores = {}
    for attack in enabled_attacks:
        result = attack_results.get(attack)
        if result is None or result.get('error'):
            continue  # exclude failed attacks
        raw_scores[attack] = extract_score(attack, result)
        # extract_score reads the canonical score field per Section 1

    if not raw_scores:
        return {'NIST_CRS': 0, 'status': 'no_results'}

    # Step 2: Normalise each raw score
    normalised_scores = {}
    for attack, S in raw_scores.items():
        thresh   = SAFE_THRESHOLDS[attack]
        critical = CRITICAL_THRESHOLD
        N = max(0.0, min(100.0, (S - thresh) / (critical - thresh) * 100))
        normalised_scores[attack] = N

    # Step 3: Weighted sum
    weighted_sum  = sum(WEIGHTS[a] * normalised_scores[a] for a in normalised_scores)
    total_weight  = sum(WEIGHTS[a] for a in normalised_scores)
    NIST_CRS      = weighted_sum / total_weight

    # Step 4: Risk classification
    if   NIST_CRS < 25: risk_level = 'LOW'
    elif NIST_CRS < 50: risk_level = 'MEDIUM'
    elif NIST_CRS < 70: risk_level = 'HIGH'
    else:               risk_level = 'CRITICAL'

    # Step 5: Pass/Fail tally
    pass_fail = {a: (raw_scores[a] < SAFE_THRESHOLDS[a]) for a in raw_scores}
    pass_count = sum(pass_fail.values())
    fail_count = len(pass_fail) - pass_count

    # Step 6: Worst attack
    worst_attack = max(normalised_scores, key=normalised_scores.get)
    worst_score  = normalised_scores[worst_attack]

    # Step 7: Score breakdown (for the bar chart in Score Breakdown panel)
    score_breakdown = [
        {
            'attack': attack,
            'raw_score': raw_scores[attack],
            'normalised_score': normalised_scores[attack],
            'pass': pass_fail[attack],
            'weight': WEIGHTS[attack],
            'contribution': WEIGHTS[attack] * normalised_scores[attack] / total_weight,
        }
        for attack in normalised_scores
    ]
    score_breakdown.sort(key=lambda x: x['normalised_score'], reverse=True)

    # Step 8: Priority action list (see Section 5)
    priority_actions = build_priority_actions(attack_results, raw_scores, pass_fail)

    # Step 9: Cross-attack comparison table (see Section 4.5)
    comparison_table = build_comparison_table(attack_results, raw_scores, normalised_scores, pass_fail)

    return {
        'NIST_CRS':        round(NIST_CRS, 1),
        'risk_level':      risk_level,
        'pass_count':      pass_count,
        'fail_count':      fail_count,
        'worst_attack':    worst_attack,
        'worst_score':     round(worst_score, 1),
        'score_breakdown': score_breakdown,
        'priority_actions': priority_actions,
        'comparison_table': comparison_table,
        'raw_scores':      raw_scores,
        'normalised_scores': normalised_scores,
        'pass_fail':       pass_fail,
    }
```

### 4.8 Priority Action List

The most actionable section for NSO decision-makers. A deduplicated, severity-ordered action list synthesised from all attack recommendations.

**Algorithm to build the priority action list:**

```python
def build_priority_actions(attack_results, raw_scores, pass_fail):
    actions = []

    # === TIER 1: CRITICAL (Score > 50 on any attack) ===
    # These are dataset-release-blocking issues

    if raw_scores.get('Prosecutor', 0) > 20:
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'URGENT',
            'colour': '🔴',
            'action': f"Mitigate Prosecutor attack ({raw_scores['Prosecutor']:.0f}% risk) — highest priority",
            'detail': f"Apply record suppression for {attack_results['Prosecutor']['num_singletons']} singleton records, OR generalise {attack_results['Prosecutor']['top_qi']} to raise Min-K to {user_k}.",
            'attacks_addressed': ['Prosecutor', 'Journalist', 'Rec. Linkage', 'Singling Out'],
            'mechanism': 'k-Anonymity / Record Suppression'
        })

    if raw_scores.get('Marketer', 0) > 20:
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'URGENT',
            'colour': '🔴',
            'action': f"Mitigate Marketer attack ({raw_scores['Marketer']:.0f}% risk) — highest priority",
            'detail': f"Expected {attack_results['Marketer']['expected_correct_reids']} bulk re-identifications. Apply k-anonymisation (target Min-K ≥ {user_k}) and enforce l-diversity for all sensitive attributes.",
            'attacks_addressed': ['Marketer', 'Rec. Linkage'],
            'mechanism': 'k-Anonymity + l-Diversity'
        })

    if raw_scores.get('Attr. Disclose', 0) > 20:
        worst_sa = attack_results['Attr. Disclose']['worst_sa']
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'URGENT',
            'colour': '🔴',
            'action': f"Mitigate Attribute Disclosure attack ({raw_scores['Attr. Disclose']:.0f}% risk)",
            'detail': f"ADR = {attack_results['Attr. Disclose']['overall_adr']:.1%}. Enforce l-diversity ≥ {user_l} for {worst_sa}. {attack_results['Attr. Disclose']['guaranteed_records']} records are in fully homogeneous ECs — their {worst_sa} value is directly inferable without re-identification.",
            'attacks_addressed': ['Attr. Disclose', 'Inference', 'Model Inversion'],
            'mechanism': 'l-Diversity'
        })

    if raw_scores.get('Differencing', 0) > 20:
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'URGENT',
            'colour': '🔴',
            'action': f"Mitigate Differencing attack ({raw_scores['Differencing']:.0f}% risk) — requires Differential Privacy",
            'detail': f"DDR = {attack_results['Differencing']['DDR']:.1%}. {attack_results['Differencing']['exact_count']} records are exactly reconstructable via aggregate query pairs. k-anonymity and l-diversity DO NOT protect against this. Apply Laplace mechanism (ε=1.0) to all published statistics derived from this dataset.",
            'attacks_addressed': ['Differencing'],

    if raw_scores.get('Model Inversion', 0) > 30:

            'attacks_addressed': ['Model Inversion', 'Inference', 'Attr. Disclose'],
            'mechanism': 'l-Diversity + t-Closeness + Noise'
        })

    if raw_scores.get('Singling Out', 0) > 5:
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'URGENT',
            'colour': '🔴',
            'action': f"Mitigate Singling Out attack ({raw_scores['Singling Out']:.0f}% risk)",
            'detail': f"{attack_results['Singling Out']['num_singletons']} records can be singled out using ≤ 3 QI columns with NO external data required. This violates GDPR Article 4(1) and DPDP Act 2023 data minimisation principles. Generalise or suppress [top_singling_column].",

        })

        actions.append({
            'priority': 'HIGH',
            'colour': '🟠',

            'attacks_addressed': ['Inference'],
            'mechanism': 'l-Diversity + QI Generalisation'
        })

    if raw_scores.get('Membership', 0) > 5:
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'MEDIUM',
            'colour': '🟡',
            'action': f"Mitigate Membership Inference ({raw_scores['Membership']:.0f}% high-risk records)",
            'detail': f"{attack_results['Membership']['num_high_risk']} records are outliers — their distinctive profile alone enables presence confirmation. Apply top/bottom-coding and attribute generalisation for the most distinctive records.",
            'attacks_addressed': ['Membership'],
            'mechanism': 'Outlier Suppression / Top-Bottom Coding'
        })

    if raw_scores.get('Journalist', 0) > 5:
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'MEDIUM',
            'colour': '🟡',
            'action': f"Note: Journalist attack ({raw_scores['Journalist']:.0f}% risk) — population-adjusted re-ID risk",
            'detail': f"Journalist Re-ID Risk is {raw_scores['Journalist']:.1f}% vs Prosecutor risk of {raw_scores.get('Prosecutor', 0):.1f}%. Sampling provides some protection, but {attack_results['Journalist']['num_population_unique']} records remain unique even at population level.",
            'attacks_addressed': ['Journalist'],
            'mechanism': 'k-Anonymity / Sampling Control'
        })

    if raw_scores.get('Rec. Linkage', 0) > 5:
        actions.append({
            'priority': 'HIGH',
            'colour': '🟠',
            'action': f"Mitigate Record Linkage attack ({raw_scores['Rec. Linkage']:.0f}% expected linkage rate)",
            'detail': f"ECLR = {raw_scores['Rec. Linkage']:.1f}%. Amplification Factor = {attack_results['Rec. Linkage']['amplification_factor']}×. An attacker with an external voter roll can bulk re-identify {attack_results['Rec. Linkage']['expected_correct_reids']} records. Generalise {attack_results['Rec. Linkage']['top_qi']} first.",
            'attacks_addressed': ['Rec. Linkage', 'Marketer'],
            'mechanism': 'k-Anonymity / QI Generalisation'
        })

    # Sort: URGENT → HIGH → MEDIUM → LOW
    priority_order = {'URGENT': 0, 'HIGH': 1, 'MEDIUM': 2, 'LOW': 3}
    actions.sort(key=lambda x: priority_order[x['priority']])

    return actions
```

## 5. Key Metric Extraction: `extract_score()` Implementation

This function reads the canonical score field from each attack's result dictionary:

```python
def extract_score(attack_name, result):
    """Returns the raw 0-100 score for the given attack from its result dict."""

    score_map = {
        'Prosecutor':     lambda r: r.get('re_id_risk', 0) * 100,
        # re_id_risk = num_singletons / N (as fraction), already 0-1 from Prosecutor spec
        # NOTE: Some specs define this as mean(link_scores); use num_singletons/N for badge

        'Journalist':     lambda r: r.get('re_id_risk_journalist', 0) * 100,

        'Marketer':       lambda r: r.get('marketer_reid_rate', 0) * 100,

        'Singling Out':   lambda r: r.get('predicate_so_rate', 0),
        # predicate_so_rate is already a percentage (0-100) in Singling Out spec

        'Inference':      lambda r: r.get('overall_form_a_risk', 0) * 100,

        'Membership':     lambda r: r.get('pct_high_risk', 0),
        # pct_high_risk = (high_risk_records / N) × 100

        'Rec. Linkage':   lambda r: r.get('ECLR', 0) * 100,

        'Attr. Disclose': lambda r: r.get('overall_adr', 0) * 100,

        'Differencing':   lambda r: r.get('DDR', 0) * 100,

        'Model Inversion':lambda r: r.get('MIRisk_dataset', 0) * 100,
    }

    extractor = score_map.get(attack_name)
    if extractor is None:
        return 0
    try:
        score = extractor(result)
        return max(0.0, min(100.0, float(score)))
    except Exception:
        return 0
```

## 6. Score Validation & Sanity Checks

The Comparison module must assert these invariants after computing scores. Log a warning (visible in the developer console, not in the user UI) if any fail:

| Check | Assertion | If Failed |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| C1 | `journalist_score ≤ prosecutor_score` (always, same dataset) | Log warning: "Journalist > Prosecutor — check ec_size_population computation" |
| C2 | `marketer_score == prosecutor_score` approximately (same ECLR formula) | Acceptable small difference due to population prior adjustment; no action |
| C3 | `NIST_CRS ∈ [0, 100]` | Clip to bounds; log error if Weighted Sum / Total Weight is outside [0,1] |
| C4 | All `Nᵢ ∈ [0, 100]` after normalisation | clip() function in algorithm handles this |
| C5 | If `pass_count + fail_count == n` | Basic count check — ensures no attacks dropped silently |
| C6 | If all attacks return score = 100, `NIST_CRS = 100` exactly | Verify weighted average with equal weights |
| C7 | `rec_linkage_score ≈ prosecutor_score` (same ECLR formula, different framing) | No action needed; expected approximate equality |

## 7. State Management and Re-Run Behaviour

### 7.1 When to Recompute

The Comparison module must **recompute automatically** whenever:
- The user clicks "Run Assessment" and at least one enabled attack produces new results
- The user enables or disables an attack in the left panel (attack scenarios checkboxes)
- The user changes QI/SA selection or k/l/t parameters (these trigger a stale-data warning)

### 7.2 Stale Data Warning

If the user modifies QI/SA/k/l/t configuration AFTER running attacks (but before re-running), show:

```
⚠️ Configuration has changed since the last run. Comparison scores below reflect 
the PREVIOUS run's QI/SA/k/l/t settings. Click "Run Assessment" to refresh.
```

If only some attacks have run (e.g., user ran 6 of 10), the Comparison module computes the composite over the 6 completed attacks and shows:

```
Showing composite for 6/10 attacks. Run all attacks for a complete score.
Missing: [list of not-run attacks]
```

## 10. Key Design Decisions and Rationale

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Weighted average over simple mean** | Prosecutor, Marketer, and Model Inversion represent more severe, legally significant threats. A simple mean would treat Journalist and Membership equally with these — misleading for policy decisions. |
| **Threshold-relative normalisation (Nᵢ)** | Inference's "safe zone" ends at 40%, not 5% like re-ID attacks. Without normalisation, Inference at 45% raw would contribute less to the composite than Prosecutor at 10% raw — but the Prosecutor at 10% is actually safer (below its threshold). |
| **6-Axis Radar over 10-Axis** | 10 separate axes creates visual clutter and false precision. The 6 conceptual threat dimensions group attacks by mechanism, making it immediately clear whether the dataset's problem is re-identification, attribute leakage, or presence detection. |
| **Priority Action List deduplication** | Prosecutor and Record Linkage share the same fix (k-anonymity). A naive merge of both attack's recommendations would show the same action twice. The Priority Action List merges overlapping recommendations and tags which attacks each fix addresses. |
| **Journalist always ≤ Prosecutor** | Enforced by the Journalist algorithm (`ec_size_population ≥ ec_size_sample`). The Comparison module sanity check (C1) catches any implementation bug where this invariant breaks. |
| **Differencing gets a separate urgent warning** | It is the only attack not fixed by any of the other privacy mechanisms. NSO officers may assume "if we fix k-anonymity and l-diversity, we're safe" — the Comparison module must make the DP requirement explicit and non-optional. |
